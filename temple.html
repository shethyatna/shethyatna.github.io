TEMPLES OF JAINISM



.RANAKPUR JAIN TEMPLE

The Ranakpur Jain Temple is a white marble temple complex in Ranakpur, Rajasthan, India. It was built in the 15th century by Darna Shah, a Jain businessman. The temple is dedicated to Adinath, the first Tirthankara of Jainism.Also it has the most intricate carvings and the best atmosphere.



PALITANA JAIN TEMPLE

The Palitana Jain Temple is a complex of over 900 temples located on Shatrunjaya Hill in Palitana, Gujarat, India. It is considered to be the most sacred Jain pilgrimage site in the world. The temples were built over a period of 900 years, starting in the 11th century.The Palitana Jain Temples are dedicated to various Tirthankaras, or spiritual leaders of Jainism. The main temple is dedicated to Rishabhdeva, the first Tirthankara. The temples are made of white marble and are decorated with intricate carvings. You have to go through 3800 steps to reach the temple . The temple was made by kumarpal. He was an Indian king from the Chaulukya dynasty of Gujarat. He ruled present-day Gujarat and surrounding areas. He was a great devotee to jain religion.



.GIRNAR JAIN TEMPLE

The Girnar Jain Temple is a complex of Jain temples located on Mount Girnar in Junagadh, Gujarat, India. It is considered to be one of the most important Jain pilgrimage sites in the world. The temples were built over a period of centuries, starting in the 11th century.The Girnar Jain Temple complex consists of over 20 temples, dedicated to various Tirthankaras, or spiritual leaders of Jainism. The most important temple in the complex is the Neminath Temple, which is dedicated to the 22nd Tirthankara, Neminath. The Neminath Temple is a large and imposing structure, made of black granite and decorated with intricate carvings.To reach the Girnar Jain Temple complex, pilgrims must climb over 9,999 steps. The temple is made by Sajjana, the governor of Saurashtr.



.SHIKHARJI JAIN TEMPLE

Shikharji Jain Temple, also known as Sammed or Sammet Shikharji, is one of the holiest pilgrimage sites for Jains, located in Giridih district, Jharkhand, India. It is situated on Parasnath hill, the highest mountain in the state of Jharkhand.The current structure of temples at Shikharji was re-built by Jagat Seth in 1768 CE. However, the idol itself is very old. The Sanskrit inscription at the foot of the image is dated 1678 CE. One of the shrines dates back to the 14th century.it would take 12000 steps for reaching the temple.



.MOUNT ABU JAIN TEMPLE

The Dilwara Jain Temples, also known as Delvada Temples, are a group of five Jain temples located about 2.5 kilometers from the Mount Abu settlement in Sirohi District, Rajasthan, India. The earliest were built by Bhima I and supposedly designed or at least financed by Vastupala, Jain minister of Dholka. They date between the 11th and 16th centuries, forming some of the most famous monuments in the style of MƒÅru-Gurjara architecture, famous for their use of a very pure white marble and intricate marble carvings.



.JIRAWALA JAIN TEMPLE

The Jirawala Jain Temple is a renowned Jain pilgrimage site located in the village of Jirawala in the Sirohi district of Rajasthan, India. It is situated around 58 kilometers from Abu Road. The temple is dedicated to Lord Parshwanath, the 23rd Tirthankara of Jainism.The temple is believed to be over 2,800 years old and has been an important Jain center for centuries. It has received patronage from many Jain acharyas and kings over the years. The iconic idol of Lord Parshwanath, the principal deity of the temple, was found during an excavation in the 9th century CE.The Jirawala Jain Temple complex is a sprawling one, spread over an area of over 100 acres. The main temple is a magnificent structure with intricate carvings and sculptures. It houses the idol of Lord Parshwanath, which is made of white marble. The temple complex also includes several other temples, shrines, and gardens.



.SHANKHESHWAR JAIN TEMPLE

The Shankheshwar Jain Temple is a white marble temple dedicated to Lord Parshwanath, the 23rd Tirthankara of Jainism. It is located in the Shankheshwar district of Gujarat, India. The temple is one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Jains all over the world.The temple was originally built in the 11th century, but has been renovated and expanded several times over the centuries. The current structure was built in the 13th century by Vastupala and Tejpal, two Jain ministers of the Chaulukya dynasty.



.PAWAPURI JAIN TEMPLE

The Pawapuri Jain Temple of Mahavir Swami is a highly revered temple dedicated to Lord Mahavira, the 24th and last Tirthankara of Jainism. It is located in the holy city of Pawapuri in the Nalanda district of Bihar, India. Pawapuri is also known as Apapuri, which means a town without sins.The temple is situated in the middle of a tank filled with red colored lotus flowers. It is believed that Lord Mahavira attained Nirvana (salvation) in Pawapuri in 527 BC. The temple was originally built by King Nandivardhan, Mahavira's elder brother.The main deity of the temple is a white marble statue of Lord Mahavira. The statue is seated in a meditative posture on a lotus pedestal. The temple also houses a sacred relic of Lord Mahavira, known as the Charan Paduka, or foot impression. The amazing thing of the same is that every diwali the golden shed over the god spins without any motor or human interference.

.NAGESHWAR JAIN TEMPLE

The Nageshwar Parshwanath Jain Temple is one of the most important and revered Jain temples in Madhya Pradesh, India. It is located in the Unhel town of the Jhalawar district, on the border of Madhya Pradesh and Rajasthan. The temple is dedicated to Parshvanatha, the 23rd Tirthankara of Jainism.The Nageshwar Parshwanath Jain Temple is believed to be over 1,200 years old, and it is one of the oldest Jain temples in Madhya Pradesh. The temple was renovated in 1207 AD by Jain Acharya Shri Abhay Devsuri. The temple is built in white marble, and it is a beautiful example of Jain architecture.The main attraction of the Nageshwar Parshwanath Jain Temple is the 14-foot-tall statue of Lord Parshvanatha.



.NAKODAJI JAIN TEMPLE

Nakodaji Jain Temple is a major Jain pilgrimage site in the Indian state of Rajasthan. It is located between the villages of Vikrampura and Nakoda in Barmer District. The temple complex is dedicated to the 23rd Tirthankara, Parshvanatha, and also houses a shrine to Nakoda Bhairava, a folk deity revered by both Jains and Hindus.The temple was originally built in the 3rd century BCE, but the current structure was constructed in the 11th century CE. It has been renovated and added to over the centuries, and now features a sprawling complex of courtyards, shrines, and other structures.The main temple is a five-story structure with a black marble idol of Parshvanatha in the sanctum sanctorum. The idol is adorned with precious stones and jewels, and is said to be highly miraculous.
